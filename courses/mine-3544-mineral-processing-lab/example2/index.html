Final Project Proposal Changes

Due 3/29/2020 on Canvas

Project Description

Revised Guide

The audience of the proposal has become more broad. Your supervisor at your job is still an audience member, but you will be contracting out the laboratory work. The proposal will also serve as a document the contractor will use to inform themselves on how to conduct experiments. Major changes to the Mineral Processing Experimental Report Guide are as follows:

  1. Technical Approach

    a. Purpose

…Knowledge of the audience is critical to balancing detail. For example, the writer does not necessarily need to prescribe the step-by-step instructions that will be followed; however, the writer should clearly explain what parameters will be tested, particularly those that will influence experimental results.

In a separate subsection under Technical Approach you will need detailed, listed, instructions for the contractor. In order to create this list you will need to contact the contractor to determine their laboratory capabilities, and their estimated time to get you the results. The details on this list are very important, someone from any science or engineering background should be able to perform the experiments if given this list. See the Revised Guide for an example.

In a separate list from the procedure, you will need to specify the specific documentation details, such as pictures, you would like to have. Remember, the contractor is very busy and has many customers that ask for different details. After giving the contractor the experimental design, they will give you back a report. In order to clearly communicate what you would like from them, consider section 2.d..

  1. Resources and Timeline

    a. Purpose

…This section should describe the supplies, equipment, and reagents that will used during testing. Furthermore, this section should include a small calendar or Gantt chare showing when key elements of the project will be completed. Items to include in the calendar include: experimental work time for the lab to complete experiments, data analysis and figure generation, writing and technical conferences with the course instructor, and final report preparation. Students should carefully consider how much time will be needed to complete each task and integrate this knowledge into the planned timeline.
