Lab 3 Particle Size Analysis

Lab 3

Hints

Fitting the G-S equation

The G-S equation is:

$$ Y = \left(\frac{X}{k}\right)^m $$

Lets simplify this, so that $X$ and $Y$ represent what we see when we do sieve analysis.

$$ \mathrm{Frac. Pass} = \left(\frac{\mathrm{Size}}{k}\right)^m $$

We need to linearize the G-S equation to use the line fitting formulas. We do this by taking the natural log.

$$ \ln(\mathrm{Frac. Pass}) = m\ln(\mathrm{Size}) - m\ln(k) $$

Therefore in the linear formula $y = mx + b$ we have eqivalently:

$$ \begin{aligned} y &= \ln(\mathrm{Frac.Pass})\\\
m &= m\\\
x &= \ln(\mathrm{Size})\\\
b&= -m\ln(k) \end{aligned} $$

We know that in order to solve for the slope we calculate the Covariance of $x$ and $y$:

$$ s_{x,y} = \sum_i^n \frac{(x-\bar{x})(y-\bar{y})}{(n-1)} $$

as well as the variance of $x$

$$ s_x^2 = \sum_i^n \frac{(x-\bar{x})^2}{(n-1)} $$

Now, since $x = \ln(\mathrm{Size})$ it is important to note that $\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_i^n\ln(\mathrm{Size_i})$ or more simply the average of all the $\ln(\mathrm{Sizes})$

Next we combine the variance and covariance equation:

$$ \begin{aligned} \mathrm{Slope} &= \frac{\sum_i^n \frac{(x-\bar{x})(y-\bar{y})}{(n-1)}}{\sum_i^n \frac{(x-\bar{x})^2}{(n-1)}}\\\
&= \frac{\sum_i^n (x-\bar{x})(y-\bar{y})(n-1)}{\sum_i^n (x-\bar{x})^2(n-1)} \end{aligned} $$

Let’s simplify this further to make it less confusing:

$$ \mathrm{Slope} = \frac{\sum_i^n (x-\bar{x})(y-\bar{y})}{\sum_i^n (x-\bar{x})^2} $$

A final hint:

If you plot $\ln(\mathrm{Frac. Pass})$ vs. $\ln(\mathrm{Size})$ as you did in the spreadsheet and use excell to fit a line, the slope you see on the line will be equal to $m$ in the G-S equation. You can use that to check your work.
